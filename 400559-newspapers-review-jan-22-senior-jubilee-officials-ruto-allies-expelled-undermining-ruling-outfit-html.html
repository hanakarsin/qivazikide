Newspapers review for Jan 22: Senior Jubilee officials want Ruto expelled for undermining ruling par

The Kenyan newspapers on Friday, January 22, reported on the continued wrangles in the ruling Jubilee Party ahead of the 2022 General Election.

The race to succeed Council of Governors chairman Wycliffe Oparanya has began in earnest with the election set for next week. The contest is set to pit pro-handshake supporters on one side and the Tanga Tanga faction on the other.

1. Daily Nation

Senior Jubilee officials allied to President Uhuru Kenyatta now want Deputy President William Ruto and his allies expelled from the party over their association with the recently launched United Democratic Alliance (UDA).

Even though the the new party (formerly the Party for Development and Reforms) is part of the Jubilee coalition as it had signed a pre-election deal, Jubilee vice chairman David Murathe argued it was illegal for it to field candidates against the position of ruling outfit.

"Those leaders including the DP who are pushing the UDA agenda will lose their seats. Things are cooking. Just wait," Murathe confidently said on Thursday, January 21."When a party that is supposed to be a coalition partner fields candidates against our people, its members cannot claim to be part of us. May be it's time to revisit the coalition agreement," he added.

The only lawmaker to be elected under PDR, Fatuma Dullo, who is the Isiolo senator said she was not consulted on the change of name.

2. The Standard

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) is rushing against time to put its house in order as the 2022 General Election inches closer.

The Opposition party is set to invite applications from candidates seeking presidential ticket and its National Elections Board has been tasked with the responsibility to recruit its flagbearer.

The latest development came after ODM chairman John Mbadi confirmed the outfit's party leader Raila Odinga would be on the race to State House.

The party fears it is falling behind in the race to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta as some aspirants led by Ruto have already hit the ground running.

3. People Daily

Council of Governors chairman Wycliffe Oparanya's stint is set to come to an end next week opening a new battleground for Uhuru and Raila on one hand and DP Ruto on the other.

The council's chief executive officer Jacqueline Omogeni confirmed the election to succeed the Kakamega county boss was on.

"The election has been slated for January 29, we will as usual go for consensus but if it fails, we will have a vote," Omogeni stated.

Some of the county chiefs who have expressed interest for the chair's seat are Jackson Mandago (Uasin Gishu), Kivutha Kibwana (Makueni), Anne Waiguru (Kirinyaga) and Salim Mvurya (Kwale).

4. Taifa Leo

The Independent, Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) on Thursday, January 21, was pressed to explain why the names of Uhuru and Raila were missing in the list of 3.1 million supporters who appended their signatures for the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

The duo were the first Kenyans to append their signatures in support of the 2020 Constitutional amendment Bill at the Kenyatta International Conference Centre in November 25, 2020.

Other big names that missed on the list were those of Amani National Congress leader Musalia Mudavadi, trade unionist Francis Atwoli, Jubilee Secretary General Raphael Tuju and David Murathe.
