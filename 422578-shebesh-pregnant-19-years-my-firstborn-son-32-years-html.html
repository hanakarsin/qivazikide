Shebesh Says She Got Pregnant at 19 Years: &quot;My Firstborn Son Is 32 Years Old&quot;

  • Frank and Rachel Shebesh met over 30 years ago, and their love is still as strong as the day the first day
  • The fact that Shebesh is married outside her tribe, she said her parents had no problem with that
  • In an interview with Churchill, the Chief Administrative Secretary (CAS) in the Ministry of Public Service and Gender praised the love of her life, describing him as a great and down-to-earth man

Celebrated politician Rachel Shebesh is happily married to one man, and they have three sons together. They have been together for over 30 years.

In an interview on Churchill Show, the former Nairobi County Woman Representative talked candidly about her husband, Frank Sbebesh.

The 50-year-old politician revealed that she got pregnant after Form Four.

"I got pregnant for my husband when I was 19 years old. My firstborn son is 32 years old. I never regret having him despite stopping my education for a while, which I also didn't mind," she said.

The Chief Administrative Secretary (CAS) in the Ministry of Public Service and Gender praised the love of her life, describing him as a great and down-to-earth man.

"My husband is extremely humble. He is from Khwisero, Kakamega county. Great man, beautiful soul," the mother of three said.

Shebesh also revealed that her husband doesn't like being in the limelight and lives a basic life.

"Anapanda matatu, basi hata saa zengine anaenda Khwisero na basi unamuuliza surely unaingia kwa basi? Anasema 'why not? Watu wengine wanaingia kwa basi' (He uses public transport matatu and buses. At times he even boards a bus to upcountry and when you ask him why he says he is the same as other people who use public transport)," she said.

Adding:

"He gives us grounding."

Rachel Shebesh's 50th birthday

Last month, Shebesh celebrated her 50th birthday in a colorful party graced by female politicians, among them Governor Anne Waiguru and Karen Nyamu.

Waiguru and Nyamu were spotted having a good time dancing to the Tanzanian hit song Sukari while celebrating Rachel Shebesh's birthday.

The all-white event was the talk of the town.
