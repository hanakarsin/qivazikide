Jakoyo Midiwo: Former Gem MP Dies Aged 54 after Long Illness

Former Gem MP Jakoyo Midiwo is dead.

TUKO.co.ke understands the former lawmaker died on Monday, June 14, according to information by Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) Director of Communications Philip Etale.

Etale made the announcement on his Twitter and asked Kenyans to pray for the family and friends of the politician who passed on after a long illness.

The ODM party also confirmed the news of Midiwo's demise and eulogised him as a gallant son of the soil and a defender of the party who never shied away from speaking the truth.

"Sad day for our party. We have just lost a gallant son of the soil, a founder member of the Change Movement and a three-time Member of Parliament for Gem Hon. Jakoyo Midiwo. "He was a defender of the party and never shied away from speaking the truth. To the family, we say pole sana," the statement from ODM indicated.

A report by Citizen TV indicated the former lawmaker passed away just days after his sister's death who is set to be buried on Saturday, June 19.

Condolence messages

Politicians and Kenyans at large took to their social media platforms to mourn the departed leader who served as the Gem MP from 2002 to 2007 under Narc and 2013 to 2017 under ODM.

Former Kiambu Governor William Kabogo said:

"I mourn my friend and colleague Hon. Jakoyo Midiwo. Fare thee well my friend. My heartfelt condolences to his family and friend."

Governor Anyang Nyongo said:

"I'm extremely saddened by the shocking death of my friend- former Gem MP Washington Jakoyo Midiwo. Unbelievable!"

Black Monday

News of Midiwo's death emerged just hours after renowned Nairobi industrialist Christopher John Kirubi died.

Kirubi, who had been in and out of the hospital, had a long battle with cancer since 2017 when he was diagnosed with the disease.

The news of his demise was rife on social media on Monday, June 14, before his family later confirmed the sad news.
