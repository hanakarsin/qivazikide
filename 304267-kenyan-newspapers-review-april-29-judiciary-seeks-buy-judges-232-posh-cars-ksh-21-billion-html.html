Kenyan newspapers review for April 29: Judiciary seeks to buy judges 232 posh cars at KSh 2.1 billio

Kenya's dominance in the London Marathon was given wide coverage by newspapers on Monday, April 29.

The dailies also reported on graft accusations leveled by Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko against ex-gubernatorial candidate Peter Kenneth and nominated MP Maina Kamanda.

1. Standard

The Standard reported that the Judiciary has forwarded a KSh 5 billion budget to the National Assembly for approval.

In the budget, KSh 2.1 billion will be used to buy 232 highend cars for High Court Judges.

Among the vehicles are 80 Mercedes Benze E200, 41 Chevrolet Trailblazers, 70 Ford Rangers and 41 other Mercedes Benz.

The remaining KSh 2.9 billion will be injected into other areas of priority that include judicial staff's mortgages, pensions and car loans.

2. Star

Nominated MP Maina Kamanda has accused Governor Mike Sonko of seeking cheap publicity and acting childishly.

Kamanda was responding to fiery allegations made against him by Sonko that he has grabbed public land and erected structures.

The MP said he bought the land from a person who was being threatened by land grabbers and after that, he used the due process to acquire it.

Kamanda was shamed alongside Peter Kenneth whom the flamboyant governor accused of sending KenyaRe to its death bed.

3. People Daily

The Kenya Police Airwing department has once again been put on the spot over alleged misuse of funds.

People Daily reported that the department has been bagging KSh 1.2 million weekly for fuel but ironically, nearly all its aircraft are grounded due to technical reasons.

The department has been utilising the finances despite the fact that no aircraft with troops have been making air surveillance on Boni forest and Lamu.

Earlier, acquisition of five aircraft sparked controversy after it emerged the planes were refurbished from Ukraine and not brand new US machines as claimed.

4. Taifa Leo

Taifa Leo reported that Kiambu Governor Ferdinand Waititu has been put in a fix over the new-found relationship between his predecessor William Kabogo and DP Ruto.

On Sunday, April 28, Kabogo threw his weight behind Ruto and assured him of massive support from Mount Kenya region as he pursues his 2022 presidential bid.

The ex-governor's support to the DP has surprised many since Kabogo was once a fierce critique of Ruto.

Waititu, however, remains the only governor from the region who has consistently expressed confidence in Ruto and has been accompanying him to different events.

5. Daily nation

Members of parliament have accused the National Treasury and KRA of setting unrealistic budgets leading to big deficits.

Led by Kikuyu MP Kimani Ichung'wa, the MPs said deficits contributed to what they termed as ballooning appetite for loans.

The MPs want borrowed money to be put in viable projects and this should not include running of ministries.

Despite setting serious strategies to manage the KSh 5.3 trillion public debt, the law makers said they want the debt not to go beyond 50% of GDP.
