Ken Mijungu Mourns Death of Close Uncle, Months after Losing Sister: Definition of Heartbreak

  • Ken Mijungu has mourned the death of his uncle, Martin Okoo Ngaji, who died at the age of 61
  • The late Martin formerly worked in the office of the president in the Internal Affairs Department
  • The journalist noted he was heartbroken as his death came just six months after his sister's loss

KTN news anchor Ken Mijingu is mourning the death of his beloved uncle.

The journalist has had a difficult 2021 with his beloved sister's loss and is now going through another casualty in the family.

Mijungu went on his Instagram Stories on Tuesday, September 21, to announce that his uncle Martin Okoo Ngaji had passed away.

The newspaper obituary shared by the journalist on social media confirmed that his uncle passed away on September 7, at the age of 61.

The late Martin formerly worked in the office of the president, Internal Affairs Department, but it did not disclose what led to his demise.

The late survived by a wife and four children, one deceased.

Mijungu remembers late sister

In another post, Mijungu shared a photo of his aforesaid uncle with his late sister, Affline Agiro Ngaji, at an event together.

The media personality said the picture reflected the real definition of heartbreak after losing both in just six months.

"Definition of heartbreak. Six short months and they are both gone," he wrote.

Mijungu mourns sister

In early March, the TV host wrote an emotional text online alongside a video of him and the late sister dancing at a party while he held the selfie camera.

In the post, Mijungu said his young sister's sudden death hit him harder than that of his dad.

He revealed the nickname he had given her and wondered "where baby sisters go when they leave so sudden".

He wrote:

"This one hit me harder than my dad's death! I called her Jaber inside, she is no more. When your baby sisters leave so sudden, where do they go?"

From the video, one could see how joyful and vibrant the sister was, and that is something to remember her by.

The news anchor shared the same video on his Instagram stories and wrote a simple message: "R.I.P Siz".
