Video of CS Mwangi Kiunjuri dancing to hitsong Tetema with a boy sparks controversy

A video of Agriculture CS Mwangi Kiunjuri dancing to Tetema song with a boy has caused a stir online.

Surfacing just hours after the hitsong was banned by Kenya Film Classification Board (KFCB) chair Ezekiel Mutua from being played in public, netizens could not help but spice up the controversy with hilarious comments.

Kiunjuri shared the video clip on his Facebook page on Tuesday, August 27, capturing the events that transpired during Laikipia Awards 2019 held at the Nanyuki Sportsmans Arms, to reward young talented artistes in the county.

" If you are young and talented, it is like you have wings. Believe in yourself," Kiunjuri wrote on his Facebook page

The jovial CS took to the stage with the boy alongside other guests and started making Tetema moves that left the audience cheering.

The boy who danced next to the CS demonstrated good understanding of the song and his moves always matched with the tune, stealing the show attended by hundreds of guests.

His dancing mates including Kiunjuri were forced to constantly keep an eye on him to steal some skills and keep up with the pace and tune.

"I believe talent can't be taught but it can be awakened and that is why I am committed to helping young people develop their talents," said the CS

Netizens who watched the clip shared varied views.

To others, the song and dance was a shame and the CS got it wrong particularly when he involved the kid.

The KFCB chair banned Tetema and Wamlambez songs from being played outside clubs saying the hits contained a lot of sexual content.
