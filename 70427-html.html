I Don't Regret Dumping APC For PDP Femi Fani-Kayode

Former Aviation Minister Femi Fani-Kayode on Tuesday in Osogbo said that he does not regret his decision in returning to the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP).

Fani Kayode

Fani-Kayode speaking with newsmen said it was better for him to “return home’’ because he did not like the way the All Progressives Congress (APC) was being handled.

The former Minister said: “Moreover, some of the issues that initially took me out of the PDP have already been addressed.

“I am back in the PDP for good, the APC is a party being managed by two individuals,” Fani-Kayode alleged.

Meanwhile speaking on the forthcoming Aug. 9 Osun State governorship election, the Ile-Ife-born politician said he was confident of PDP's victory saying the party “will win convincingly’’.

He also stated the people of Ife would vote massively for PDP’s candidate, Sen. Iyiola Omisore, who he described as a true son of Ile-Ife.

He said: “This is an opportunity for the people of Ife to have their own person at the executive level in the state.

“Omisore was a Deputy Governor, my father was a Deputy Prime Minister in the old Western region.

“This time around, we are going for the highest executive level in Osun because Omisore has what it takes to be governor.”

Femi Fani-Kayode who is his known for his expressing his opinion on controversial subjects would be recalled dumped APC for the ruling party, Peoples’ Democratic Party (PDP) on June 2, 2014.

It was reported on June 30, Femi Fani-Kayode had accused the APC of having links with the dreaded Boko Haram sect, saying the APC’s leaders are being supportive and sympathetic of the dreaded sect as it suits their purpose of destabilizing the country and discrediting the PDP-led government.

On June 9, Femi Fani-Kayode had threatened to expose APC’s atrocities.
