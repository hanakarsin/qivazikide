Ousted Wajir Governor Abdi Forced out of CS Matiang'i's Meeting Attended by Incumbent Ahmed Muktar

  • Incumbent Ahmed Muktar protested that his seat had been taken by the ousted Wajir Governor Abdi Mohamud
  • The meeting was between county governors and Matiang'i's National Development implementation and communication cabinet committee (NDICC)
  • During the introduction time, Mohamud and the Muktar (current governor) stood up and introduced themselves as the governors of Wajir

Impeached Wajir Governor Mohamud Abdi was, on Wednesday, June 30, ejected from a meeting called by Interior Cabinet Secretary (CS) Fred Matiang’i.

Two governors

The meeting was between county governors and Matiang'i's National Development Implementation and Communication Cabinet Committee (NDICC).

Mohamud is said to have been removed from the meeting after confusion during the introduction time when Muktar (current governor) and him stood up and introduced themselves as Wajir governors.

The embarrassing incident caused a bit of confusion in the meeting, forcing the organisers to call police officers to intervene.

Officers were instructed to remove the embattled Mohamud from the room.

He, however, calmly left the Kenya School of Government premises where the meeting was being held.

At the beginning of the meeting, Muktar protested that his seat had been taken by the ousted govenor who arrived earlier.

Impeachment of Mohamud

Earlier on, TUKO.co.ke reported that the Wajir County Assembly impeached Mohamud on April 25.

This was after Wajir MCAs accused him of gross misconduct, abuse of office, corruption and other fraud-related charges.

The senate-select committee chaired by Nyamira senator Okong'o Omogeni presided over the matter and found the county boss guilty of the charges.

Muktar, the deputy govenor, was later sworn in as the govenor on Tuesday, May 18.

He took the oath of office to become the third governor in a ceremony attended by the local political leadership.

On Friday, May 28, the Council of governors threw weight behind the impeached Wajir county boss Mohamud after rejecting newly elected Governor Muktar.

The council claimed Mohamud was still in charge as the governor of Wajir.

Addressing media on Thursday, May 27, the Council of Governors noted that Mohamud will remain in the office until the case filed in the High Court for his removal is heard and determined.

The Council of Governors chairman Martin Wambora expressed his dissatisfaction with how Muhamud was impeached.

“The council notes the orders by the High Court that reaffirmed the position of Wajir Governor Mohamed Mohamud. His case is to be affirmed by the Court of Appeal,” Wambora said.
