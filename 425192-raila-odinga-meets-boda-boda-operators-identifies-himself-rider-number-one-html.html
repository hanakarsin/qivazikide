Raila Odinga Meets Boda Boda Operators, Identifies Himself as &quot;Rider Number One&quot;

  • ODM party leader Raila Odinga met a section of boda boda operators and self-help groups from Nairobi
  • He assured them of his support and commitment to address their plights once he occupies the top office on the land
  • Raila also called on the groups to shun being capitalised on by selfish politicians as the 2022 General Election draws closer

On Monday, September 6, ODM leader Raila Odinga had a session with a cohort of Nairobi boda boda operators and other self-help groups who shared with him their plight.

The former prime minister promised to push for a favourable tax regime to ensure that small entrepreneurs thrive in their different ventures.

Raila also credited his handshake with President Uhuru Kenyatta for creating a conducive environment for business and enabling considerable growth in the key sectors of the economy.

"Boda Bodas too can become a paying employer and lucrative business if a conducive environment can be created for operators to do their work, that’s Raila Odinga’s dream for the young men and women," he stated.

As he seeks to succeed Uhuru, Raila seems to have changed his approach and the way he politicks.

Lately, he has been pronouncing his commitment to empowering the youths in the event that he succeeds Uhuru as Kenya's fifth president.

The same tact has been employed by Deputy President William Ruto who has been taking credit for the youth empowerment conversations that have been witnessed in the recent past.

While Ruto vouches for the bottom-up economic model, Raila is for the energising of the rural economy, a policy he says will give the youths great fodder to empower themselves.

Copying Ruto?

As he tries to woo the young people to back his fifth presidential bid, Raila has been accused by some quarters of leveraging on a plan initially coined by Ruto.

The quarters argue that Raila has not had at heart the plight of the young people, and his move to align himself with the perceived Ruto's plan is a hoax, they say.

"Mumeona heri mucopy how Hon Ruto does his things (You have preferred to copy how Ruto does his),""Conversation has changed welcome to hustlers nation where real issues affecting comon mwanchi are being discussed,abuses will never add value to our suffering," Chemoe.

Uhuru, Raila donate KSh 3 million to Boda Boda

In late last year, hundreds of Boda Boda operators in Kisii county held a meeting with Uhuru and Raila.

The riders had sent out a request through Governor James Ongwae to have a one-on-one meeting with the two leaders to discuss their welfare.

During the meeting which was held at the Kisii golf grounds, Uhuru and Raila handed over KSh 3 million to the governor to be given to the riders.

"I will leave KSh 3 million with the governor so that you can distribute amongst yourself. However, I must say this. Think about what I have told you and if you think it is okay, invite me at the launch of your Saccos. Go organise yourselves and invite me. Are we together?" Uhuru told the crowd.
