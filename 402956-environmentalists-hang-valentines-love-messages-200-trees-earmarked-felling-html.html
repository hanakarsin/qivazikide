Over 200 trees, out of which 13 are indigenous and exotic assorted species, are earmarked for cutting to pave way for development projects.

It is against this backdrop that members of the Daima Coalition chose to celebrate Valentine's day by hanging love hearts on the selected trees.

The love hearts carried mind-triggering messages that hope to change the minds of those hellbent on felling them to save them as part of conservation.

Loggerheads between the Kenyan government and environmentalists have been commonplace, dating as far back as 1989 when the late Prof Wangari Maathai led women to strip naked at Uhuru Park.

The bold move not only put a stop to the government's plans to construct the Kenya Times Media Complex inside the park, but also won her the Nobel Peace Prize.

“We support development that is socially just and climate-smart. That means, the infrastructure development of today should have the least impact on carbon emissions and it must benefit the majority of the citizens,” said the executive director of the Green Belt Movement Nyaguthii Chege.
