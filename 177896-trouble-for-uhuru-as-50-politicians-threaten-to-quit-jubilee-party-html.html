50 politicians threaten to quit Jubilee Party

- Over 50 political aspirants from Mount Kenya region have threatened to quit President Uhuru Kenyatta's yet-to-be-launched Jubilee Party

- The politicians, mostly from Nyeri county, feel that the Jubilee Party nominations would favour the incumbent leaders

There is discontent in President Uhuru Kenyatta's yet-to-be-launched Jubilee Party.

Over 50 political aspirants from Mount Kenya region have expressed fears that the Jubilee Party nomination would favour the incumbent political leaders.

The politicians said they would seek other alternatives if the Jubilee Party nomination process would not be all-inclusive and handled democratically.

The Jubilee Party's first delegates’ conference would be held on September 10.

President Uhuru Kenyatta had made it clear that no one would be favoured during the Jubilee Party nomination.

The president bashed some politicians who are going around using his name soliciting for political mileage during the official announcement of the merger of 12 parties to form the Jubilee Party.

He said the power lies with Kenyan’s who have the liberty to decide who to choose or not.

Uhuru assured all aspirants that the party would uphold democracy during the nominations. He even indicated that the exercise carried out by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission.

To avoid any fallout after the nominations, Uhuru promised to give the losers government jobs if he gets re-elected in 2017.

He also dismissed complaints by opposition leaders Raila Odinga (CORD) and Musalia Mudavadi (ANC) that the merger of various parties previously under the Jubilee coalition to form the JP is a threat to democracy in the country.
