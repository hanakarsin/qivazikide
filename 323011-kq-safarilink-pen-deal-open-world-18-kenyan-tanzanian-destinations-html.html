KQ, Safarilink deal to open up 18 famous safari destinations to global travellers

- International visitors will be able to book Safarilink flights to beach destinations within the country and Tanzania via KQ

- Destinations covered include Amboseli, Samburu, Lamu, Zanzibar, Ukunda, Vipingo, Kilimanjaro, Tsavo, Lodwar, Kitale and Lake Naivasha

- The deal will cushion travellers from tiring and cumbersome road transport to connect to holiday destinations

- Ticket sale will effectively start in the course of the coming weeks before Saturday, November 30

Safari airline has signed a one way codeshare agreement to open up Kenya and Tanzania's safari destinations to global travellers by selling Safarilink’s direct flight tickets.

Under the arrangement, KQ will sell 18 remote Safarilink destinations in their global sales and distribution network, enabling them to combine their travel experience between respective international destinations and the routes that Safarilink operates.

Speaking during the launch, Kenya Airways CEO Sebastian Mikosz hailed the deal as one that will make it easier for international visitors access the region and bolster tourism sector in Kenya.

“As part of our strategy to continue growing the network, we have seen the need to give travel ease to our customers by connecting their destinations. This partnership will also significantly open up Kenya’s tourist destinations to international travellers by allowing tour operators and travel agencies to create unique holiday packages connecting from JKIA to Wilson Airport and eventually to their respective safari destinations, which is a great convenience to them,” said Mikosz.

The national carrier will place its codeshare flight numbers on the Safarilink flights so that customers from international destinations can book their full safari experience directly via KQ holidays or via tour operators and travel agents.

It will provide daily scheduled flights hence convenient access to the eleven airstrips in Maasai Mara and direct flights to Amboseli, Samburu, Lamu, Zanzibar, Ukunda, Vipingo, Kilimanjaro, Tsavo, Lodwar, Kitale, Lake Naivasha, Laikipia, Lewa downs, Olpajeta, Loisaba, Nanyuki and Mt Kenya.

Travellers will, therefore, avoid long and cumbersome road transport.

Safarilink Aviation boss Alex Avedi said his airline was honoured to have been selected by KQ to offer seamless travel to global visitors.

“We are very pleased to be entering into this significant partnership with KQ which will provide seamless connection of passengers from the KQ network straight into Kenya and Tanzania’s world famous parks. We are delighted that KQ chose us on account of our commitment to safety and quality,” said Avedi.

Sales will effectively start in the coming weeks and before Saturday, November 30.

This codeshare agreement comes as KQ marked its first anniversary of nonstop hauls to New York.

Other KQ international destinations include Bangkok, Dubai, Guangzhou, Mumbai, Amsterdam, London, Geneva, Rome and Paris.

Over 1.3 million people toured Kenya through JKIA in 2018 with Kenya Airways being responsible for 60% of overall traffic at the airport.
