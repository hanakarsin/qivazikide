ODM strips Governor Nanok of vice chairman position

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) has replaced tTurkana Governor Josphat Nanok as the party vice chair after holding a consultative meeting to deliberate on the matter.

Nanok's position was filled by Loima MP Jeremiah Lomorukai after the National Executive Council (NEC) unanimously resolved to effect the changes.

In a tweet shared by the Orange party on Thursday, June 27, the new vice chair was welcomed by the outfit leader Raila Odinga and will now be part of the NEC.

"Following numerous public pronouncements that he has quit the ODM party, the NEC has unanimously resolved to replace Josphat Nanok as the party vice chairman with Loima MP Jeremiah Lomorukai. He was welcomed to the NEC by the party leader," stated ODM.

While the Raila-led political outfit did not give details about what informed the decision, TUKo.co.ke understands that Nanok has been working closely with Deputy President William Ruto in recent times and this could have angered the party leadership.

The former Council of Governors chair has also been advancing the DP's 2022 presidential ambitions and publicly opposing the Orange party agenda.

He recently criticised Raila's call for a referendum stating the idea was ill-conceived and should be opposed.

The Turkana county boss who was addressing a gathering in his home turf in the presence of Ruto said those pushing for reforms were power-hungry and should not be allowed to have their way.

Reacting to the remarks, the Orange party chairman John Mbadi said the move by Nanok to support DP was expected and and did not have any consequences to ODM.

"There is nothing new that has happened, some of the people serving their second and final term are looking for future political survival. Some want to be remembered through appointments in government," he said

The move to axe him from the party leadership was expected considering the bad blood that exists between ODM leader and Ruto.
