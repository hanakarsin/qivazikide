17-year-old Boy Creates Remote-Controlled Excavator Prototype From Wood, Aluminium

  • A talented boy named Hope Emmanuel has built an excavator prototype using wood and aluminium
  • The 17-year-old Akwa Ibom indigene stated that he acquired materials used in making the machine for N200 (KSh 53)
  • Emmanuel who had gone viral in 2019 for building a similar machine said that the excavator prototype took him 2 years to create

Hope Emmanuel has become an internet sensation for building an excavator prototype using aluminium and wood.

The yellow-painted machine built by the young talented lad is remote-controlled.

The Akwa Ibom indigene who had built a similar machine in 2019 told BBC News Pidgin that it took him two years to complete the project.

Hope creates whatever he imagines

While revealing that getting the body parts needed in building the machine cost him N200 (KSh 53), the 17-year-old said that he didn't learn or wasn't taught how to create things.
