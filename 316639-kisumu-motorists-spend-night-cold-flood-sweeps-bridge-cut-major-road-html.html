Motorists spend night in the cold after floods sweep away bridge, cut off Kisumu-Kisii road

Motorists were left stranded for more than 10 hours on the Kisumu-Kisii highway following heavy rains and floods.

The raging flash floods swept away a bridge at Kodada trading centre in Rachuonyo East, Homa Bay county, rendering the usually busy road impassable.

A section of the road that was destroyed was reportedly still under construction by a Chinese company which some of the road users blamed for their woes.

Rachuonyo East police commander, Charles Barasa, advised the motorists to use alternative routes, warning the area was likely to experience more flash floods because of the ongoing rains.

Barasa further clarified the ongoing construction was being done to replace another bridge which had also been damaged by floods.

“Motorists who depend on this road must not use it where there are floods. Constructors have assured the road users of more reinforcements on the bridge to avoid risks. There will be no more spillovers,” he said on Saturday, September 28.

The road was impassable for over 10 hours, completely paralyzing transport and forcing some of the travellers to spend night in the cold.

The region has been experiencing heavy downpour which has not only affected travellers but also the area residents, especially those in the flood-prone areas.
