Ex-Nairobi Speaker Beatrice Elachi takes up new role in Uhuru's Cabinet

- Beatrice Elachi resigned on August 11, 2020, ending her protracted legal and administrative tussle with the county MCAs

- President Uhuru Kenyatta appointed her as the chief administrative secretary in the Ministry of Public Service and Gender Affairs

- The president reminded all the new Cabinet members to prioritise completion of the ongoing government projects in their respective dockets

President Uhuru Kenyatta has witnessed the swearing in of the nine newly appointed chief administrative secretaries at the State House.

The Friday 19 ceremony which was presided over by the Head of Public Service Joseph Kinyua saw the former Nairobi county assembly Speaker Beatrice Elachi take up her new role.

Elachi, who was one of the surprise appointees announced on Wednesday, February 17, was named the chief administrative secretary in the Ministry of Public Service and Gender Affairs.

Speaking during the function, Uhuru reminded the new Cabinet members to prioritise completion of the ongoing government projects in their respective dockets before the end of his term in 2022.

Uhuru said the changes were meant to foster operational efficiency, institutionalise and expedite various ground-breaking reforms.

He also indicated the changes will make the ministries and state departments more effective and better able to deliver on their mandates.

Elachi resigned after a protracted legal and administrative tussle pitting her against former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko and a section of ward reps.

The former Dagoretti North parliamentary aspirant had never known peace ever since she got appointed to lead the assembly in September 2017 as her tenure was mostly characterised by drama and chaos.
