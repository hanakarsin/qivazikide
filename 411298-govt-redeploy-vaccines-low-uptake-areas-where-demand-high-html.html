Gov't to Redeploy Vaccines from Low Uptake Areas to Where Demand is High

- There were people in certain parts of the country who were hesitant to take the vaccine, forcing the government to redeploy the vaccines

- Health CS Mutahi Kagwe said the government would not allow the vaccines to expire

- A total of 911,515 people have so far been vaccinated against COVID-19 countrywide

The National government plans to redeploy COVID-19 vaccines from the low uptake areas to regions where the demand is high in country.

Health Cabinet secretary Mutahi Kagwe divulged that about 200,000 doses were yet to be issued out, noting that the margin was due to slow uptake in some regions where people were still hesitant to take the vaccine.

Speaking in Nyeri county on Friday, May 7, the CS reiterated that the government would not allow the vaccines to expire.

"As I had said last year, if we treat COVID -19 normally it will treat us abnormally, that was true then and it is still true now," the CS said.

Kagwe also said that a total of 911,515 people had been vaccinated against COVID-19 countrywide.

"The new variants will come into the country no matter how much effort is put in, unless we close our borders completely, that is the only way," he added.

Some 911,515 people have been vaccinated so far, out of which 531,540 are aged 58 years, 160,468 are health workers, and 76,578 are security officers.

Kagwe also announced that 568 more people had tested positive from a sample size of 9,029 samples tested in the last 24 hours, marking a positivity rate of 6.3%. This now brings the country’s total caseload to 162,666.

Earlier on, the Ministry of Health announced that the COVID-19 variant from India had been detected in Kenya after 10 patients tested positive in Kisumu.

Some of the patients were in contact with foreign nationals who had been confirmed to have tested positive for the deadly Indian COVID-19 variant.

Local legislators said the government should have done more to ensure that all Kenyans are protected against the deadly virus.

Through the department of health, the county government of Kisumu has so far placed over 100 people in quarantine.
