Diploma in Primary Education: requirements, application, intake

Are you interested in pursuing a course that will lead you to become a primary school teacher? A Diploma in Primary Education is what you need. Fortunately, all the Teacher Training Colleges and some universities offer this course.

In the past, any person interested in being a primary school teacher was required to enroll in a course in P1. However, that is no more. In line with the curriculum changes being witnessed, the government through the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development has developed a course that will see trainees receive relevant skills to guide pupils on the new curriculum.

Diploma in Primary Education

The government introduced a Diploma in Primary Education to phase out the P1 course. For this new course, qualified teacher trainees will only specialize in three subject areas preferable from the following clusters.

Cluster one

The subjects under this category include:

  • Kenya sign language
  • Indigenous languages
  • Foreign languages (French, German, Chinese and Arabic)

Cluster two

The subjects here are:

  • Mathematics
  • Home Science
  • Agriculture
  • Science and Technology

Cluster three subjects

Only two subjects fall under this category. They are:

  • Social Studies
  • Religious Education (CRE, HRE, IRE)

Cluster four subjects

The subjects here are:

  • Music
  • Art and Craft

Mandatory subjects

The following are the compulsory subjects:

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Physical and Health Education
  • Kenya sign language for hearing impairment

To be approved, teachers must have completed the required hours of course work and passed the Kenya National Examination Council's exams.

How long does a Diploma in Primary Education take?

Earning a Diploma in Primary Education is a practical first step towards becoming a primary school teacher. A diploma program will typically take three years to complete, according to the approved curriculum designs.

In this period, a student will gain the foundational knowledge and skills necessary to pursue a professional career in education.

What are the entry requirements for a Diploma in Primary Education?

To enrol in this course, an applicant must meet a particular set of requirements. Here are the Diploma in Primary Education qualifications.

  • At least C (Plain) in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination (KCSE) and a C in two art subjects or languages.
  • Be a holder of Kenya Advanced Certificate of Education (KACE) with one principle and two subsidiaries.
  • At least C (Plain) in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination (KCSE) with a C- in two art subjects or languages plus a P1 teacher certificate.

When is the diploma in primary education intake? Application is currently ongoing for May 2021 Intake. Interested and qualified students are encouraged to apply.

Universities offering Diploma in Primary Education

A considerable number of universities have included this course within their programs. These institutions include;

  • Kisii University
  • Chuka University
  • Embu University
  • Zetech University
  • Maasai Mara University

How to apply for Diploma in Primary Education

The application process is straightforward. If you meet the entry requirements for primary school teaching, you can apply either online or directly to the Teacher Training Colleges of your choice.

Diploma in primary education online application involves the following steps.

  • Visit the KUCCPS portal.
  • Click on Login and then enter your KCSE index number.
  • Enter your KCSE year.
  • Enter your birth certificate number or KCPE index number as the password.
  • Apply as per your area of interest or qualification.
  • Wait for communication from KUCCPS.
  • Proceed to download the letter if the application was successful.
