Aden Duale doing good job as backbencher than when he was majority leader - Junet Mohamed

- There was a heated debate in National Assembly over appointment of MPs to House committees

- MP Junet Mohamed who talked after Aden Duale said he had received requests from MPs asking to be appointed in lucrative committees

- Junet told the MPs there was no need to fight over committees and it did not matter where one was serving

- To drive his point home, Junet used Duale as an example saying he had made a great contribution in the debate despite being a backbencher

Suna East MP Junet Mohamed has left members of the National Assembly in stitches after telling them Garissa Township MP Aden Duale has been doing a good job as a backbencher than when he was a majority leader.

Speaking on the floor of the House on Wednesday, July 15, Junet said he had observed tremendous improvement in the former majority leader since he became a backbencher.

Junet was contributing in a debate on the appointment of members to committees which had sparked reactions from MPs and was speaking after Duale had contributed.

The minority whip claimed he had received requests from some MPs who wanted to be appointed in lucrative committees.

While arguing that it doesn't matter which committee one is appointed to, Junet said the hard work of an MP could easily be noted once they put their best foot forward.

"With my experience in this house, I know if a member is appointed to any committee and he or she is hardworking, the results will be seen, it doesn't matter whether they were appointed to a committee of their academic qualifications," he said.

To drive his point home, Junet used Duale as a perfect example who he said has now been making better contributions in the National Assembly than he used to do when he was majority leader.

"I have so little to add because I can see the former majority leader has spoken and Mr. Speaker I can see he can do better as a backbencher than where he was before."Today, he has made very good contributions, I didn't know that sometimes being in the back like he is can be of great value," said Junet.

Duale was ousted from his position on Thursday, June 26, following an unforgiving purge in the Jubilee Party. He was replaced by Kipipiri MP Amos Kimunya.

The purge was then extended to the house committees which saw majority of the Tanga Tanga members being removed from committees and some reassigned.
