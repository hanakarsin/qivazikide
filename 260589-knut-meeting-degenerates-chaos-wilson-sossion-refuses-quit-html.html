KNUT meeting degenerates into chaos after Wilson Sossion refuses to quit

- Prison warders were forced to intervene after a meeting called by Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT) degenerated into chaos

- A section of the delegates wanted KNUT Secretary General Wilson Sossion to relinquish his position as ordered by the government

- Sossion has declared he will not step down following the order by Labor cabinet secretary Phylis Kandie

Security officers were on Thursday, December 14 forced to swing into action after a teachers' meeting degenerated into a war zone after KNUT secretary general Wilson Sossion refused to quit.

The Jubilee administration is determined to kick out the fiery trade unionist who was nominated to the National Assembly by Orange Democratic Movement (ODM).

A section of KNUT officials especially from the coastal region, who support embattled chairman Mudzo Nzili who leans towards Jubilee, vowed not to rest until Sossion leaves.

But Sossion has rubbished the government order saying it has no legal basis.

"I am not going anywhere. This is a trade union not a government entity," he said noting that only delegates can decide if he stays or not.

The storm was kicked off after Labour Cabinet Secretary Phyllis Kandie ordered 11 trade unionists who assumed elective leadership both in National and county assemblies to relinquish their union posts.

“It is notified for general information that the following persons, being State Officers as per provisions of Article 77 and 260 of the Constitution, cease to hold office immediately as trade union officials in their respective unions,” Kandie said in special Gazette notice on Wednesday, December 13.
