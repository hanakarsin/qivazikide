Moses Kuria promises to deactivate social media, quit alcohol as new year resolution

- Moses Kuria has been one of the most vocal politicians on social media sites albeit controversially

- Most of his posts have been causing a stir and public uproar over sensitive issues he used to lay bare

- The lawmaker said he will not make any post this year and will only remain active on WhatsApp

- Known for his love for alcohol, the MP also promised to divorce the brown bottle going foward

Every time Kenyans usher in a new year, they often make resolutions and outline some guiding principles which they anticipate to live by throughout the year.

Aware of this trend, Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria on his part has promised to deactivate all his social media accounts and quit alcohol as part of his new year resolutions.

The vocal MP made the declaration on his Facebook page on the eve of January 1, 2020, and said he would take a break from social media buzz and focus on other things.

The lawmaker said he will only be available on WhatsApp until 2021 when he will consider posting a thing on his other pages.

"Happy new year 2020 good people. For those who thought they know me. I am deactivating my Social Media accounts (except Whatsapp) throughout 2020. See you in 2021. This is my last post of the year, I am off alcohol throughout the year 2020. Tukutane kwa ground (let us meet on the ground)," he posted.

Kuria's resolution to take a break from social media for a whole year came as a surprise to many Kenyans considering his obsession with the platform which he has been using to hit at his critics and perceived political competitors.

More often than not, the Gatundu South MP's posts on Facebook have been causing a stir and attracting public uproar over his insensitivity to certain matters he used to lay bare.

In one of his recent posts, Kuria tore into Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru with unprintable insults that portrayed her as an incurably corrupt leader who had robbed Kenya of her bright future through the National Youth Service (NYS) scandal.

He also placed Opposition leader Raila Odinga on his online chopping board castigating him for allegedly being politically self-centred and perpetuating violence in the country.

Others who have tasted his wrath include Trade and Indusatralisation CS Peter Munya, former Kiambu governor William Kabogo and Woman Rep Gathoni wa Muchomba.
